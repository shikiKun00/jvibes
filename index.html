Japan has many interesting cultures and they still maintain this culture well, let's learn about it

Ojigi

Ojigi is a Japanese tradition of bowing which is used as a form of greeting, a sign of respect, apology, or thanks. This is an important aspect of Japanese etiquette and culture.

Ojigi is a reflection of the values of politeness, respect, and humility that are highly valued in Japanese culture. Performing ojigi correctly shows understanding and respect for Japanese culture and etiquette.

Kimono

Kimono is a traditional Japanese garment known for its elegant design and rich cultural symbolism. Kimonos are made from a variety of fabrics, including silk, cotton, and polyester.

A kimono consists of several parts that must be worn in a certain order, including special undergarments such as the juban and obi (large belt) that tie the kimono around the waist. Kimono is equipped with various accessories such as obiage (cloth worn under the obi), obijime (rope that ties the obi), and geta (wooden sandals).

Kimonos are often worn on special occasions such as weddings, tea ceremonies, festivals and other cultural events.

Japanese Bonsai

Bonsai is an ancient Japanese art that involves growing and pruning miniature trees in pots to create a shape and proportion that is aesthetically pleasing and resembles a mature tree in a small size.

Bonsai originated in China under the name "penjing," but developed into a unique art form in Japan over the centuries. The word "bonsai" itself means "potted plant" in Japanese.

Bonsai is a hobby that requires precision, patience, and dedication, and provide satisfaction in creating works of art that live on develop and change over time.

Tokyo

Tokyo is the capital of Japan and one of the largest cities in the world with a population of more than 13 million people. Tokyo is the center of technology and Japanese culture. Areas such as Ginza are famous for their luxury shopping centers, while Akihabara is known as Japan's electronics and pop culture district.

Kyoto

Kyoto is one of the historical cities in Japan and is famous for its rich cultural heritage. It was the capital of Japan for more than 1,000 years before Tokyo, and the city is maintained as a center of traditional Japanese culture, art and architecture.

The city is also famous for traditional Japanese festivals such as Gion Matsuri, one of the largest festivals in Japan which is held every July.

Ohanami

Ohanami, also known as hanami (Ëä±Ë¶ã), is Japanese tradition to enjoy the beauty of cherry blossoms which is blooming. Hanami means "flower viewing" in Japanese. This tradition dates back to the Nara period (710-794 AD) and became popular during the Heian period (794-1185 AD).

Ohanami is usually held in spring, when the cherry blossoms bloom, usually from late March to early April.

Parks and public places throughout Japan are popular locations for ohanami. Some famous places include Ueno Park and Shinjuku Gyoen Garden in Tokyo, as well as Maruyama Park in Kyoto.

Japanese Ryouri "Foods"

Japan has many traditional foods and dishes, even foods that we might consider strange. but don't worry, the food is safe and usually fresh

sushi

Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish consisting of rice seasoned with vinegar, sugar, and salt, served with various other ingredients such as seafood (raw or cooked), vegetables, and eggs.

Sushi is served with the addition of wasabi, shoyu and pickled ginger. The tradition of eating sushi is usually to dip sushi pieces in shoyu and add wasabi to taste.

sashimi

Sashimi is a Japanese dish consisting of thinly sliced raw meat from various types of seafood, such as fish, shellfish or shrimp, Sashimi is raw seafood served alone without rice.

Sashimi is a popular food choice in Japanese restaurants around the world and is one of the best ways to enjoy the authentic and fresh taste of seafood.

Japanese Ramen

Ramen is a popular noodle dish in Japan, famous for its rich broth and chewy noodles. Each region in Japan has its own unique ramen variant, such as Hokkaido ramen which uses a butter-based broth, or Okinawa ramen with thicker noodles and a different sauce.
